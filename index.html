Why I Never Cared About Tech Until After I Left Silicon Valley

Yeah, I was born in Santa Clara, California, home to massive tech companies like Apple, Google (Alphabet), eBay, PayPal, Intel, and many more. Other than learning how to type, and becoming familiar with the Microsoft Office suite of products, I never seemed to delve into computers or technology.

However, once the entrepreneurial bug hit me, I found myself learning how to build websites using What You See Is What You Get (WYSIWYG) editors like WIX, Weebly, and later Wordpress. Because my HTML and CSS skills were rudimentary, I focused heavily on the User Experience (UX) and User Interface (UI). In other words, I cared a lot about usability and user-friendliness.

As I continued down my business-oriented path, I had many adventures including:

  • Moved to NYC so my wife could attend Columbia University.
  • Worked as a remote e-commerce project manager for two different companies.
  • Discovered blockchain tech and taught myself it's language while building Decryptionary.

It quickly became apparent, if I only were a programmer, the potential in front of me was boundless.

I recently (Q4 2018) attended and completed The Tech Academy's Full Stack Developer Program with a focus on HTML, CSS, SQL, JS, and C# (using the .NET framework). With these languages in my toolbelt, I now feel equipped and ready to tackle programming. With my passion for UI/UX, emerging financial technologies such as blockchain, and my experience as an entrepreneur, I'm brimming with energy and hope.

If you're looking to partner with an ambitious individual, with a passion for blockchain technology and a focus on user-friendly design, I urge you to reach out and make yourself known. At a minimum, we'll have one damn good conversation over coffee.
